Article 26 — Common Equity Tier 1 Capital (CET1)

Common Equity Tier 1 capital consists of the highest quality capital elements of an institution.

CET1 items include:

(a) Ordinary share capital issued by the institution that meets regulatory eligibility criteria.
(b) Share premium accounts related to CET1 instruments.
(c) Retained earnings.
(d) Accumulated other comprehensive income.
(e) Other disclosed reserves.

For COREP supervisory reporting, CET1 capital shall be reported in template C01.00 under row 010.

---

Article 28 — CET1 Instruments Eligibility

Capital instruments shall qualify as Common Equity Tier 1 instruments only where the following conditions are met:

• Instruments are issued directly by the institution.
• Instruments are fully paid up.
• Instruments are perpetual in nature.
• Distributions are fully discretionary.
• Instruments absorb losses on a going concern basis.

Eligible CET1 instruments contribute to Own Funds and must be reported in COREP template C01.00.

---

Article 36 — CET1 Deductions

Institutions shall deduct from Common Equity Tier 1 items:

• Intangible assets.
• Deferred tax assets dependent on future profitability.
• Significant investments in financial sector entities.

These deductions must be reflected in COREP Own Funds template adjustments to CET1 capital.

---

Article 51 — Additional Tier 1 Capital (AT1)

Additional Tier 1 capital consists of subordinated capital instruments that meet regulatory criteria.

AT1 instruments include:

• Perpetual subordinated bonds.
• Contingent convertible instruments (CoCos).
• Instruments with discretionary coupons.

AT1 capital shall be reported in COREP template C01.00 under row 020.

---

Article 52 — AT1 Eligibility Conditions

AT1 instruments must:

• Be perpetual.
• Have no incentive to redeem.
• Permit cancellation of distributions.
• Absorb losses via conversion or write-down.

Eligible instruments qualify for inclusion in Tier 1 capital.

---

Article 62 — Tier 2 Capital

Tier 2 capital includes subordinated liabilities with minimum original maturity of five years.

Examples include:

• Subordinated term debt.
• Loan capital instruments.

Tier 2 capital is reported in COREP template C01.00 under row 030.

---

COREP C01.00 — Own Funds Template Instructions

Template C01.00 captures the composition of regulatory Own Funds.

Key rows include:

010 — Common Equity Tier 1 Capital  
020 — Additional Tier 1 Capital  
030 — Tier 2 Capital  
050 — Total Tier 1 Capital  
060 — Total Own Funds

Institutions must ensure totals reconcile with component capital items.

---

COREP Validation Rules — Own Funds

The following validations apply:

• Tier 1 Capital = CET1 + AT1.
• Total Own Funds = Tier 1 + Tier 2.
• CET1 must be ≥ 0.
• Capital instruments must meet eligibility criteria.

Validation breaches must be flagged in supervisory reporting submissions.

---

PRA Rulebook — Own Funds General Requirements

Firms must maintain sufficient Own Funds to meet prudential requirements at all times.

Own Funds calculations must:

• Reflect fully paid capital instruments.
• Deduct ineligible items.
• Align with CRR eligibility rules.

Supervisory returns submitted via COREP must accurately reflect regulatory capital composition.

---

Audit & Traceability Requirement

Institutions must maintain documentation demonstrating:

• Source regulatory rules.
• Instrument classification rationale.
• Mapping of capital instruments to COREP fields.

Supervisors may request evidence supporting reported Own Funds figures.
